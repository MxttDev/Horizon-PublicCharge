SASP Public Charges

Miscellaneous Felony
Charge Licenses Definition
Accessory to Felony Anyone who attempts to help or aid someone in the preparation, act or escape of a felony offence.
Aircraft Hijacking Unlawfully gaining control over an aircraft, forcing the manoeuvres of the pilot or operating the aircraft themselves.
Burglary Unlawfully entering into a dwelling or private premises and committing a criminal act while on said property.
Escaping Lawful Custody Fleeing from the lawful custody of a law enforcement officer.
Felony Evading Arrest Impound Vehicle While operating a motor vehicle or on foot, intentionally fleeing from a law enforcement officer attempting lawful arrest or detainment.
Human Trafficking The transportation, transfer, harbouring or receipt of a person(s) against their will.
Illegal Gambling Hosting or organising games/activities that involve a gambling or betting element with no valid Gambling Permit for said game/activity.
Impersonation of a Lawyer or Government Official The self portrayal of a lawyer or government official in an attempt to fool another or gain access to restricted areas. Or, the operation of a governmental-issued vehicle with the usage of emergency equipment.
Jailbreak The act of attempting to or successfully freeing someone else from custody/prison.
Perjury The act of willfully and deliberately providing false information while under oath.
Theft of Emergency vehicle The theft of an emergency vehicle. This includes Fire service, EMS, Law enforcement & Lifeguard.
Trespassing on Airport Property Unauthorized entering or remaining upon restricted government/private property that is utilized as an airport, airfield, runway, landing strip or helipad. This includes Los Santos International Airport, Sandy Shores Airfield, Grapeseed McKenzie Airfield and Cayo Perico Airstrip.
Trespassing with the Intent of Theft Intering or remaining upon restricted government/private property in which you can reasonably prove the intent of theft. This can include tools to aid in breaking entry.
Witness Intimidation The threatening of crucial court witnesses by pressure or extortion to compel them to not to testify.
Violent Crimes
Charge Licenses Definition
An act of Terrorism Requires Judge Authorization The act of creating mass destruction via explosive destruction or mass shootings in highly populated areas to assert political or ideological control or fear into the public.
Armed Robbery The act of depriving someone of or actively taking their property through the threat of violence in the presence of a weapon.
Arson The criminal act of intentionally setting fire to a property.
Assault Threat of bodily harm coupled with an apparent, present abillity to cause the harm.
Assault with a Firearm Revoke Weapons License The use of a firearm for the purpose of making violent threats against another person.
Attempted Mass Murder Revoke Weapons License Taking substantial steps to end the life of three or more people, or taking action towards a group of three or more people deliberately or recklessly with extreme disregard for human life. Only stackable via warrents.
Bank Robbery The act of or direct involvement in stealing property from a bank, including removing property from the vault.
Battery An interntional unpermitted act causing harmful or offensive contact with another (includes hitting a person or spitting on them).
Commercial Break In The act of planning and acting upon the breaking into a commercial property.
Kidnapping Remove Weapons License The Unlawful movement and detainment of a person against their will from one location to another. Weapons license is removed if weapon is involved
Murder Remove Weapons License Murder is the unlawful killing of a human being (not revived by EMS/LEO) with malice aforethought. Weapons license is removed if weapon is involved
Store Robbery The theft of money from a shop through the use of violence or force.
Vehicular Manslaughter Revoke Driving License Causing the unintentional death of a person or other driver through reckless, dangerous or negligent driving.
General
Charge Licenses Definition
Animal Cruelty The deliberate act of abusing, neglecting or otherwise harming a non-human creature.
Assisting An Offender Anyone who attempts to help or aid someone in the preparation, act or escape of a crime.
Brandishing a Weapon Revoke Weapons License | Weapon seizure The act whereby an individual holds, points, waves or carries out other means of gesture with a item or firearm capable of causing harm.
Breach of the Peace A public disturbance, or an act considered like to cause one.
Contempt of Court Unlimited fine TBD by Judge Failure to obey a lawful order of a court, showing disrespect for the judge, disruption of the proceedings through poor behaviour, or publication of material or non disclosed material, which in doing so is deemed likely to jeopardize a fair trial.
Criminal Damage The act of intentionally causing temporary or permenant damage to someone elses property.
Disobeying a Lawful Order The willful violation of a lawful order from a law enforcement officer.
Failure to Identify Failing to identify one's self whilst being detained on reasonable suspicion. Suspect shall be given a fine and will be detained pending confirmation of identity.
False Report Intentionally making a false statement/911 dispatch message.
Harassment To knowingly pursue a pattern of conduct intended to annoy, seriously alarm, or terrorize another person, with no lawful purpose, which causes that person to suffer substantial emotional distress.
Indoecent Exposure Intentionally exposing the private or intimate parts of one's body in a public place. Includes sexual behaviour or conduct.
Jaywalking Failure to use a crosswalk when crossing the street within the city (when one is within a city blocks distance).
Obstruction of Justice The intentionally obstruction or hindering of lawful investigation.
Obstruction of a Government Official Knowingly impeding an on duty government official (Police Officer, EMS, Lawyer) in the course of their duty.
Oepn Carry Violation Weapon seizure Long guns are prohibited from being openly carried on your person including being slung within city limits this does not include small arms that are stored inside a holster.
Poaching Weapon seizure The illegal hunting, pursuing, trapping, harassing, catching, killing, taking, transporting, or possession any portion of any wildlife, except fish, without having first procured a license from the Department of Wildlife Conservation and/or Off the Bone. Alternatively, the hunting, pursuing, trapping, harassing, catching, killing, taking, transporting, or possessing any portion of any endangered or protected wildlife listed as follows: (1) Whales, (2) Dolphins, (3) Sharks, (4) Orcas, or (5) Stingrays.
Possession of Stolen Goods Weapon seizure Acquiring stolen goods which any reasonable person would have realize have been stolen, extorted, embezzled or unlawfully obtained.
Prostitution Acquiring stolen goods which any reasonable person would have realize have been stolen, extorted, embezzled or unlawfully obtained.
Public Intoxication Being visibly drunk or under the influence of drugs in public.
Public Urination Urinating in a public place like a park, alley, or sidewalk. It is considered a public nuisance.
Resisting Arrest The act of intentionally obstructing detainment into lawful custody.
Stalking The repeated following and harassing of another person with the intent to instill fear or injury.
Threat to life To make a genuine and credible threat to kill another person.
Trespassing Entering or remaining upon restricted government/private property. This does not include public car parks.
Unlawful Discharge of a Firearm Revoke Weapons License The reckless, negiigent or unsafe use of a firearm in a public place.
Unlawful Imprisonment Does not stack with kidnapping The act of holding someone in a location against their will. This does not include transportation, but does allow for movement on the property e.g. from the garden to inside the house.
Unlicensed Event Hosting or aiding in the hosting of an illegally planned event on private property OR public property without the appropriate permits.
